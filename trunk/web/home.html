TOPPR - The Online Protein Processing Resource

Over two percent of all human and mouse genes encode for proteases. This large group of enzymes controls many biological processes and they are crucially important in relatively simple processes such as food digestion as well as in highly regulated processes such as controlled cell death. In addition, proteases add to several pathologies including cancer, cardiovascular and in inflammatory diseases. It is commonly recognized that a more detailed understanding of protease-controlled processes can be achieved by extending our overall knowledge on proteases, their (preferred) substrates and specificities.

Managing, analyzing, comparing and integrating processed events from different proteases have always been very cumbersome. Here we present The Online Protein Processing Resource (TOPPR) that stores high quality processed events and are made available in and easy and intuitive analysis platform.
